Abstract

This paper uses text analysis of publicly available unstructured customer feedback mined from the online Trustpilot project in order to (i) explore the differences in customer perceptions of finance and fintech companies; (ii) construct a predictive model of customer satisfaction using company characteristics and customer context-dependent sentiment; and (iii) offer a better way of understanding customer feedback using unstructured customer data. We find that banks and fintechs envoke orthogonal sentiments in customers in a sense that the same services are rated positively for fintechs and negatively for traditional financial institutions. We also show that our new model trained on Trustpilot data offers a significant improvement over traditional natural language models used for the same purpose.

Understanding sentiment in financial and fintech context: NLP AND predictive modelling of customer satisfaction
